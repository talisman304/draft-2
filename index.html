Fit check

Advanced, intensive training. If you are brand new, a fundamentals course may be a better first step.

Best fit if you:

Want to make your results more predictable and more aesthetic, consistently.

  • Have placed composites and want stronger anterior aesthetics.
  • Want repeatable finishing, texture and gloss control.
  • Prefer hands-on, workflow-oriented training.

Consider another route if you:

Need a slower introduction to core composite and bonding fundamentals.

  • Have never layered anterior composites.
  • Need foundational isolation and incremental build-up teaching.
  • Prefer a theory-first pace.

Is this suitable for beginners? +
It is positioned as advanced, intensive training. If you are completely new to composite veneers, a fundamentals course may be more suitable first.

How do I secure a place? +
A simple flow reduces drop-off: enquiry → confirmation of dates/fees → deposit/payment link. If possible, add an online booking/payment step.
