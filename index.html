About Dr Charles Brandon

Summary of background highlights from the official page.

Training and credentials

  • Graduated from King’s College London
  • Bachelor of Dental Surgery (BDS)
  • Trained in Brazilian veneers, Spanish orthodontics, Italian implantology and British restorative dentistry

Iridescent Dental Academy

Creator of the Iridescent training institute, teaching cosmetic dentists to a high standard with a focus on facially aesthetic driven dentistry and refining the natural beauty of teeth rather than masking it.

Program overview

The masterclass studies every stage of the smile design process: photography, digital smile design, smile try-in, preparation of pink aesthetics, placement, layering, polishing, and marketing of aesthetic dentistry.

Smile design

Photography, case planning, smile sketching, Digital Smile Design, and translating plans to clinical execution.

Layering and anatomy

Anterior composite placement: preparation, isolation, bonding, shaping and layered build-ups.

Texture and polish

Refine tooth planes and line angles, then finish surface texture and polish to a lifelike standard.

4-day course breakdown

Each cohort runs Wednesday to Saturday, 09:00–17:00, with a drinks reception on Saturday.

1
Day 1 — The theory of smile design

Smile design in real time on your own cases: photography, case planning, smile sketching, Digital Smile Design, 3D printing and palatal stent fabrication; pink aesthetics review; material selection and equipment review; occlusal planning; placement of palatal shells.

Digital Smile Design 3D printing Palatal stent Occlusal planning
2
Day 2 — Layering and tooth anatomy

Anterior composite placement masterclass: tooth preparation, isolation, bonding, shaping and layering. Create your first ten-unit case on a normal typodont and explore the nuances behind individual tooth anatomy.

Isolation & bonding Shaping 10-unit case Anatomy framework
3
Day 3 — Texturing and polishing your veneers

Refine anatomy, surface texture and polish to perfection. Focus on the details that make teeth appear lifelike. Finish your first ten-unit case to a world-class standard and begin placement on the next space-closure case.

Line angles Surface texture Polishing protocol Space closure
4
Day 4 — Creating a masterpiece

Independent polishing of your previous case; photographing and documenting your work; placement, polishing and photography of your final ten-unit veneer case on a tooth wear patient. Concludes with review of all cases, celebratory drinks reception, and provision of certificates.

Documentation Photography Tooth wear case Certificates

Requirements and policies

Key points summarised from the official course page.

Booking and attendance

  • Course fees are non-refundable.
  • To reschedule to another date, provide at least 30 days’ notice.
  • Absence/sickness must be reported 24 hours before the day of the course; additional days may be offered to repeat missed content.
  • No-notice non-attendance may incur a per-day fee of 25% of the course value when rebooking.

Equipment and accreditation

  • A significant part of the course requires an iPad, Apple Pencil and a DSLR camera.
  • If you do not bring these, no substitute is provided.
  • The course is accredited; upon completion you receive 32 hours of CPD.
  • Certification is provided only after all four days are completed.
